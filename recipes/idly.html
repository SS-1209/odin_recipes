Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake that's soft and fluffy. Here's a step-by-step recipe to make idli:

ingredients

  • For batter
    • 2 cups Idli rice (or parboiled rice)
    • 1 cup Urad dal (split black gram)
    • 1/2 tsp Fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
    • 1/2 cup Cooked rice or poha (flattened rice)
    • Salt to taste
    • Water (as needed)

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. RIse and soak
    • Rinse the idli rice and urad dal separately until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in enough water for at least 6 hours.
    • Soak the urad dal separately in enough water for at least 6 hours.
    • If using poha, soak it in water for 10 minutes before grinding.
  2. grind the batter
    • Drain the water from the urad dal.
    • In a wet grinder or a high-speed blender, grind the urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter, adding water gradually (approx. 1/2 to 1 cup of water). The batter should be light and airy.
    • Transfer the urad dal batter to a large bowl.
    • Drain the water from the rice and fenugreek seeds.
    • In the same grinder, grind the rice, fenugreek seeds, and soaked poha (or cooked rice) to a slightly coarse batter, adding water as needed (approx. 1/2 to 1 cup). The batter should have a grainy texture.
    • Mix the rice batter with the urad dal batter. Add salt and mix well.
  3. Fermentation
      4. Cover the batter and leave it in a warm place to ferment for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter will rise and become airy. In cooler climates, it might take longer to ferment.
  4. prepare idli steamer
    • Grease the idli molds with a little oil or ghee.
    • Pour the fermented batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full.
  5. steam the idlies
    • Bring water to a boil in the idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle/weight).
    • Place the idli stand in the steamer and cover with a lid.
    • Steam the idlis on medium-high heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the idli comes out clean.
    • Remove the idli stand from the steamer and let it cool for a few minutes.

Enjoy your home made idlies
